Species

Animals and plants across Earth's major biomes

Scarlet Macaw

The scarlet macaw (Ara macao) is a large, vibrant parrot native to the humid evergreen forests of Central and South America. Known for their brilliant red, yellow, and blue plumage, they are highly intelligent, social birds that mate for life and can live up to 50 years in the wild. They primarily eat nuts, seeds, and fruits.

Heliconia flower

Heliconia is a genus of nearly 200 species in the family Heliconiaceae, native primarily to tropical America, with some species found on islands in the western Pacific and Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Maluku. These perennial herbaceous plants grow from rhizomes and can range from 0.5 to 4.5 meters tall.

Red-eyed tree frog

Red-eyed tree frogs are small amphibians, with males averaging about 2 inches (5 cm) and females slightly larger at around 3 inches (7.5 cm) in length. They weigh between 6 and 15 grams. Their bodies are bright green, with blue and yellow vertical stripes along the sides, a white or cream-colored belly, and orange or red feet.

Monstera

Found in countries including Mexico, Panama, and Colombia. They flourish in environments characterized by high humidity, dappled sunlight, and nutrient-rich soil. These conditions are essential for their growth and development, allowing them to reach impressive heights, often exceeding 30 feet in the wild.

Zebra

The plains zebra (Equus quagga) is the most common and widespread zebra species in Africa, known for its distinctive black-and-white stripes, social behavior, and adaptability to grassland habitats.Zebras are primarily grazers and can subsist on lower-quality vegetation. They are preyed on mainly by lions, and typically flee when threatened but also bite and kick

Candelabra tree

Candelabra trees are native to eastern and southern Africa, including countries such as Ethiopia, Sudan, Uganda, Kenya, Angola, Malawi, Mozambique, South Africa, Swaziland, Zimbabwe, Zambia, Tanzania, and Rwanda. They thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, including savannas, dry bushveld, open woodlands, rocky slopes, and occasionally termite mounds.

African elephant

African elephants can live up to 70 years in the wild. Their trunks are multifunctional, capable of delicate tasks like picking a single leaf or powerful actions like uprooting trees. They also use their trunks for thermoregulation, spraying water and dust to cool and protect their skin. Their sense of smell is highly developed, allowing them to detect water and food from miles away.

Lion

The lion is the second biggest big cat after the tiger. Unlike most big cats, the lion is social, and lives in family groups called prides. This ferocious apex predator works together with its companions to take down sizeable prey like water buffalo, which are much larger than a single lion.
