More about us

Biomes is more than just a visual atlas—it is a gateway to understanding the living systems that shape our planet. Each biome represents a complex network of interactions between climate, geography, and life, forming distinct environments where species adapt and evolve over time. From lush forests to arid deserts, these ecosystems are essential to maintaining Earth’s environmental balance and supporting life at every scale.

Our platform explores five major biome categories, offering a deeper look into how each one functions and why it matters. These biomes—forests, grasslands, deserts, tundra, and aquatic systems—are defined by patterns such as temperature, rainfall, and biodiversity. Together, they form the foundation of global ecosystems, influencing everything from weather systems to food chains and human livelihoods.

Biomes also highlights the extraordinary diversity of life found across these environments. For example, tropical rainforests alone contain over 50% of the world’s terrestrial species despite covering only a small portion of Earth’s surface. Meanwhile, aquatic ecosystems—covering roughly 75% of the planet—support an immense variety of life, much of which remains undiscovered.

By making this knowledge accessible and engaging, Biomes aims to inspire awareness and action. Understanding biodiversity is critical, as it supports ecosystem stability, resilience, and the resources that humans depend on. Through education and exploration, we empower individuals to appreciate the natural world and take part in protecting it for future generations.
